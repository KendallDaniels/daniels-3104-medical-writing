Medical Writing

Writing made for scientists, doctors, and the public

Every day, new knowledge and information accumulate in the field of medicine by way of a limitless amount of research studies, clinical trials, and technology. All this information needs to be effectively communicated to different audiences, such as physicians, healthcare professionals, patients, and drug regulators.

Medical writing involves the creation of scientific documents by writers who are devoted to the field of medicine. They work alongside physicians and scientists and are involved in the accumulation of data and the presentation of said information in an appropriate manner. Documents such as disease or medicine-related literature, content for healthcare websites, and health-related magazines or news are created by medical writers. All of these duties make a medical writer more malleable, where they can act as an editor, journalist, or medical liaison. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, medical writing has a job outlook of 11%, which is faster than average.
