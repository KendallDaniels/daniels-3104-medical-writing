Internships

Internships are provided by companies in order to help students grow personally and professionally in the regards to their career and personal goals. In addition to gaining skills and experience, students are able to bond with a professional that they can ask for advice, while creating their own networking channels for possible future employment.

Science Writer Intern

Science News

Science News, an award-winning science magazine out of Washington D.C, is currently accepting applications for an internship in science writing. This program is filled with experience and professionalism; for, former Science News interns have worked with:

  • the New York Times
  • the Washington Post
  • Popular Science
  • Scientific American
  • Science
  • Nature
  • Discover

Responsibilities

Interns work as full-time science writers under the guidance of some editors and writers from Science News. Because they are working full time, the interns will be given $600 dollars a week. Interns are given the opportunity to report and write several articles per week, including news stories and longer features.

Qualifications

The internship is preferred for those who are planning to pursue careers in science writing. One applicant will be selected for each time period. It should be noted that those who have completed an advanced degree in journalism with an emphasis in science writing will be given first priority, as well as writers who are working toward an advanced degree in one of the sciences or experienced science-oriented journalists.This internship opportunity is offered three times a year for about a three-month stay in the following terms:

  • Spring (January to April)
  • Summer (June to September)
  • Fall (October to December)

Medical/Science Writer

Technical Resources International, Inc.

Technical Resources International, Inc. (TRI), in Bethesda, Maryland, provides a wide range of services in the realm of communications, health technology, and information technology. The company is known for their technical and communications skills, which are represented by:

  • Experienced technical and medical writers
  • Clinical research specialists
  • Registered Nurses
  • Editors
  • Information and database managers
  • Graphic designers

Responsibilities

This internship position involves preparing both scientific and regulatory documents for clients in private industries and government agencies. For instance, the company submits information to the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) by means of:

  • Investigational New Drug applications
  • Annual reports
  • Preclinical and clinical study reports
  • Safety reports
  • Data analysis

Requirements:

Requirements for the internship include an MS degree, along with 3 or more years of relevant work experience, or a Ph.D. in the fields of pharmacology, biochemistry, molecular biology, or immunology. Specifically, a background in oncology and/or immunology is highly desirable. The interns are expected to have excellent data interpretation and computer skills. This includes excellent experience with PC-based systems and Microsoft Office. It is also required that the intern has high attention to detail and is familiar with database design, FDA regulations, and clinical research.
