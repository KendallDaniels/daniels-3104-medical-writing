Science Writer Intern

Science News

Science News, an award-winning science magazine out of Washington D.C, is currently accepting applications for an internship in science writing. This program is filled with experience and professionalism; for, former Science News interns have worked with:

  • theNew York Times
  • theWashington Post
  • Science
  • Nature
  • Discover
  • Popular Science
  • Scientific American

This internship opportunity is offered three times a year for about a three-month stay in the following terms:

  • Spring (January to April)
  • Summer (June to September)
  • Fall (October to December)

    Responsibilities

    Interns work as full-time science writers under the guidance of some editors and writers from Science News. Because they are working full time, the interns will be given $600 dollars a week. Interns are given the opportunity to report and write several articles per week, including news stories and longer features.

    Qualifications

    The internship is preferred for those who are planning to pursue careers in science writing. One applicant will be selected for each time period. It should be noted that those who have completed an advanced degree in journalism with an emphasis in science writing will be given first priority, as well as writers who are working toward an advanced degree in one of the sciences or experienced science-oriented journalists.

    American Physical Society

    The American Physical Society (APS), the worldâ€™s leading association of physicists, is offering a full-time Science Writing Intern position starting Summer, 2017. The internship will be a 6-month, full-time paid position, based at APS Headquarters in College Park, MD (near DC Metro Green Line). The Science Writing Intern, under the guidance of the Head of Media Relations, will assist in creating news stories and press releases focusing on physical science topics, while developing journalism skills and assembling a portfolio of materials to establish a career in science journalism.

    Responsibilities

    Interns assist the APS Head of Media Relations in preparing press releases about research relating to physics. They also identify and summarize newsworthy journal papers, as well as APS conference presentations. At these conferences, the interns also aid in Press Room activities by supporting participating journalists and science writers. Interns also write occasional stories for:

    • APS News
    • Physics Focus
    • Inside Science News Service
    • Physics blogs and other APS internet outlets

    Qualifications

    Interns are preferred to have advanced undergraduate physics course work and/or a BA/BS in physics. In addition, it is preferred to have completed and passed writing courses or published writing for non-scientists. Journalism and English majors with minors in a physical science will also be considered. Technical and scientific writing skills that are directed towards general audiences are also appreciated.
