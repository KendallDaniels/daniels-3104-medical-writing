Certificate Programs

Certificate programs are offered in both professional and academic settings in order to show that a student has mastered a specific topic or area of study. Certificate programs are made for students who already hold an undergraduate degree, such as a Bachelor of Arts (BA) or a Bachelor of Science (BS).

American Medical Writers Association - Essential Skills Certificate

The American Medical Writers Association (AMWA) offers an Essential Skills Certificate that must be completed with 2 years. The AMWA Essential Skills (ES) certificate program aide individuals to attain a mastery of editing, writing, communication, and bibliographic skills. Earning the ES Certificate will "Enhance professional credibility, show commitment to excellence in medical communication, refine and refresh skill-sets and expertise, and maximize competitive edge". The certificate requires that the participant complete 8 credits through the following courses:

  • Basic Grammar and Usage for Biomedical Communicators
  • Punctuation for Clarity and Style
  • Sentence Structure and Patterns
  • Statistics for Medical Writers and Editors
  • Elements of Medical Terminology
  • Tables and Graphs
  • Essential Ethics for Medical Communicators
Credits may also be earned through the completion of Self-Study Workbook quizzes or by attending chapter events.

University of California San Diego Extension - Medical Writing Certificate

The University of California San Diego Extension designed a online program in order to provide college graduates with the knowledge and skills needed to work as a medical writer in many levels of expertise. The certificate gives scientists and editors with a strong biological and medical background the opportunity to write for scientific audiences, as opposed to the public or field of journalism. Only those who have received undergraduate degrees in biology, chemistry, nursing, nutrition, or public health can receive a certificate. The following courses must be completed in order to receive the certificate:

  • Introduction to Medical Writing & Editing
  • Medical Writing & Editing II
  • Designing Figures, Tables, & Graphs
  • Ethics for Medical Writers
  • CT: Medical Writing: Protocols, Reports, Summaries, and Submissions
  • CT: Understanding Oncology
  • CT: Regulation of Drugs, Biologics, Devices, and Diagnostics
  • Capstone: Journal Article and Publication Development

University of Chicago Graham School of General Studies - Medical Writing and Editing Certificate

The Medical Writing and Editing certificate program is designed to teach students the fundamentals and best implementation of medical writing and editing in society. As opposed to other programs, there are no required classes and students can pick and choose the material that work best for them, as long as they take the correct number of courses. For the regular certificate, four courses are required and for the advanced certificate, six courses are required. Classes are offered both online and in-person:

  • Introduction to Medical Editing I
  • Introduction to Medical Editing II
  • Advanced Medical Editing
  • MEDLINE and Beyond: Medical Research Databases
  • Writing in the Medical Sciences
  • Freelancing for Medical Writers and Editors
