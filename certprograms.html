Certificate Programs

Certificate programs are offered in both professional and academic settings in order to show that a student has attained mastery of a specific topic or area. Certificate programs are designed for students who already hold an undergraduate degree, such as a Bachelor of Arts (BA) or a Bachelor of Science (BS).

American Medical Writers Association

Essential Skills Certificate.

The American Medical Writers Association (AMWA) offers an Essential Skills Certificate that must be completed with 2 years.The AMWA Essential Skills (ES) certificate program is designed to help you refine your basic editing, writing, communication, and bibliographic skills. Earning the ES Certificate will enhance professional credibility, demonstrate commitment to excellence in medical communication, and refine and refresh skill sets and expertise. The certificate requires that the participant complete 8 credits through the following courses:

  • Basic Grammar and Usage for Biomedical Communicators (2 credits)
  • Punctuation for Clarity and Style (1 credit)
  • Sentence Structure and Patterns (1 credit)
  • Statistics for Medical Writers and Editors (1 credit)
  • Elements of Medical Terminology (1 credit)
  • Tables and Graphs (1 credit)
  • Essential Ethics for Medical Communicators (1 credit)
Credits may also be earned through the completion of Self-Study Workbook quizzes or by attending chapter events.

University of California San Diego Extension

Medical Writing Certificate

The University of California San Diego Extension designed a program in order to to provide college graduates with the knowledge and skills needed to work as a medical writer in the many levels of expertise. The certificate will equip scientists and editors with a strong biomedical and life science background to write for scientific audiences, as opposed to the public or field of journalism. Only those who have received undergraduate degrees in biology, chemistry, nursing, nutrition, or public health ca receive a certificate.
