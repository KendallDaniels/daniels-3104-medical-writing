Graduate Programs

Graduate Programs serve to enhance or build upon the skills and education of undergraduate students. In general, these programs provide courses that take place over three or more semesters, or about two years. Some programs are all online, all in class, or a mix of the two. There are many medical writing, medical journalism, and science writing programs across the United States; however, I chose the programs below to demonstrate the variety of degrees that are offered, as well as the courses that work best for those who are interested in medicine. It is important to note that some programs offer a Master of Science, a Master of Arts, or one is able to choose between the two.

Boston University - Master of Science/Science & Medical Journalism

Boston University takes a more journalistic approach in regards to medical writing; for, the students are expected to learn multimedia, radio, and documentary styles. Students must take a total of 40 credits over the course of three semesters.

Fall Semester:

  • Online Journalism for Graduate Students
  • Science Unbound-Writing at the Edges of Science and Society
  • Science Newswriting I
  • Science in the Crosshairs
  • Elective

Spring Semester:

  • Advanced Science Writing
  • Conflict and Commentary in Science Reporting
  • Computer Assisted Reporting Bootcamp
  • Web Magazine
  • Science Video Production

Summer Semester:

  • Professional Internship

John Hopkins University - Master of Arts/Science in Science Writing

John's Hopkins University incorporates both medicine and science, where one can choose classes that are specific to your topic of interest. The science writing students learn to translate the complicated information and trends of science, medicine, and technology into "meaningful, perceptive prose, and media that serve a vital public purpose". This program is different compared to other ones because it does not delve into creating scientific research reports, journal articles, instruction manuals, or complicated texts. Students are required to take nine courses to earn their degree, and they must choose two core courses, two workshops, two elective courses, a residency course, and a thesis.

Core Courses:

  • Techniques of Science-Medical Writing
  • Contemporary Science-Medical Writing: Creative and Professional Forms

Workshops:

  • Science-Medical Writing Workshop
  • Science Personal Essay and Memoir Workshop

Elective Courses:

  • The Nature of Nature
  • The Literature of Science

Residency Course:

  • On the Bay: Exploring the Chesapeake

Thesis:

  • Thesis and Careers in Science Writing

New York University - Master of Arts/Science in Health and Environment Reporting

This program specializes in it's networking capabilities with both companies and guest speakers. SHERP also organizes trips every year to Brookhaven National Laboratory, The New York Times, The American Museum of Natural History, and the Jamaica Bay National Wildlife Refuge. Students are also encouraged to attend annual conferences of the National Association of Science Writers, the Society of Environmental Journalists, and the Association of Health Care Journalists. The students must complete three regular semesters and a summer semester in order to complete the graduate program.

First Semester:

  • Writing and Reporting Workshop I
  • Current Topics in Science, Health and Environmental Journalism
  • Medical Reporting

Second Semester:

  • Writing and Reporting Workshop II
  • Environmental Reporting
  • Investigative Science Journalism

Summer Semester:

  • Journalistic Judgment
  • Entrepreneurial Science Journalism
  • Fieldwork in Journalism

Third Semester:

  • Science Writing
  • Elective
  • Fieldwork in Journalism
