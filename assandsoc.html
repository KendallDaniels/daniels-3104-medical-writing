Associations and Societies

Associations and Societies consist of groups of people from the same profession, who gather together for networking events and to improve the standards for the profession. Students who have just graduated and entered the field are able to network with professionals, and are praised for learning more about their field of interest. Associations provide job listings, certification classes, case studies, and journal and magazine access. Members of professional associations are student-friendly and want to mentor incoming workers as they transition to the corporate world. Associations and societies can be state-wide, nation-wide, or international.

Board of Editors in the Life Sciences

Mission: "To maintain and promote a standard of proficiency in editing in the life sciences."

The Board of Editors in the Life Sciences (BELS) was created in 1991 and is now an international association that offers credentials such as a certification of Editor in the Life Sciences, Diplomate Editor in the Life Sciences, and Honored Editor in the Life Sciences. In order to receive a certification, a three hour multiple choice exam is administered, which tests ones knowledge of scientific editing in English. To become an Diplomate Editor in the Life Sciences, one must submit and pass a formal review of edited manuscripts in a portfolio. Finally, the Honored Editor in the Life Sciences is recognized for their successful career in editing. <

Council of Science Editors

Mission: "To serve editorial professionals in the sciences by providing a network for career development, education, and resources for best practices."

The Council of Science Editors (CSE) is an international membership organization for editors who publish or wish to publish in the sciences. By promoting networking, scientific education, and scientific discussion, the counsel aids science communities of all kinds. In 1957, the organization was first known as the Conference of Biology Editors (CBE), until the name was changed to CSE in 2000. The council currently has a publication called: Science Editor. It also offers a mentorship program that requires:

  • Monthly phone calls (or as agreed upon
  • Personal meetings
  • Group activities (Including mentor/mentee training programs)
  • Webinars to present topics of interest suggested by mentors or mentees

International Society for Medical Publication Professionals

Mission: Advance the medical publication profession globally through enhanced integrity and transparency in medical publications, improved standards and best practices, education, advocacy, and professional collaborations.

Formed in 2005, the International Society for Medical Publication Professionals (ISMPP) has remained to be the only not-for-profit professional organization that is committed to the continuous support of medical publication professionals. The society has a certification program that offers medical publication professionals an opportunity to earn the recently established Certified Medical Publication Professional™. Certified Medical Publication Professional™ In order to do so, the individual must pass a psychometrically exam that tests the persons' ability to follow and promote ethical and legal standards, and to uphold the strength and duties of the ISMPP and the medical writing profession.
